# PlayCS Tournament Rulebook

**Version 1.0 — April 2026**
**Tournament Operator:** PlayCS (playcs.gg), in association with the Israeli Gaming Association

---

## Part 1: General Rules

### 1. Tournament Overview

1.1 This rulebook governs all Counter-Strike 2 tournaments operated by PlayCS ("Tournament Operator") through the PlayCS platform at playcs.gg. By registering for any PlayCS tournament, all participants confirm that they have read, understood, and agreed to abide by these rules.

1.2 PlayCS tournaments are classified as **Tier 2 Ranked Tournaments** under Valve's Tournament Operating Requirements. Tournament outcomes generate data used by the Valve Regional Standings (VRS) to determine the relative performance of Rosters.

1.3 PlayCS operates under a valid Steam Tournament License issued by Valve Corporation.

1.4 The Tournament Operator retains final authority in all matters. In exceptional cases, decisions may be made that are not explicitly covered by this rulebook in order to uphold the principles of fair play and sportsmanship.

1.5 This rulebook may be amended at any time. Material changes will be communicated to registered participants via the PlayCS platform or email. The version of the rulebook in effect at the time of the tournament's Additional Information announcement is the binding version for that tournament.

### 2. Eligibility & Registration

2.1 **Regional Eligibility.** PlayCS tournaments are open to Israeli teams. To be eligible, a team must have a majority of its roster (at least 3 out of 5 players) holding Israeli citizenship or permanent residency. This criterion is objective, transparent, and applied equally to all teams in compliance with Valve's Tournament Operating Requirements (Section 3.5).

2.2 **Age Restriction.** All participants must be at least 16 years of age at the time of registration.

2.3 **Registration.** All registration is conducted through the PlayCS platform (playcs.gg). Tournaments use open registration on a first-come-first-served basis. Registration timestamps and order are publicly visible on the tournament page for verification purposes. If a tournament reaches capacity, additional teams are placed on a waitlist (FIFO order). If a registered team withdraws, the first team on the waitlist is automatically promoted.

2.3.1 **Entry Requirements.** Depending on the tournament configuration, additional requirements may apply: identity verification (KYC), premium membership, IEA player number, and/or Akros anti-cheat. These requirements are displayed on the tournament page before registration and are enforced by the platform during the registration process.

2.3.2 **Double Registration.** A player may not be registered on more than one team in the same tournament. The platform enforces this restriction automatically.

2.4 **No Direct Invitations.** PlayCS Tier 2 Ranked tournaments do not use Direct VRS Invites. All participating rosters qualify through the open registration / open qualifier process.

2.5 **Account Requirements.** Each participant must have a valid PlayCS account linked to their Steam account. Participants may only register with one account. The use of alternate or shared accounts is strictly prohibited.

2.6 **Bans.** Players with an active competitive ban on PlayCS are not eligible to register. Any player who has received a Valve Anti-Cheat (VAC) ban in Counter-Strike may not participate. Players with VAC bans on any of their accounts must disclose this during registration; failure to do so will result in disqualification. Valve reserves the right to disqualify any player at its sole discretion.

2.7 **Team Name & Logo.** Team names and logos must not contain offensive, political, discriminatory, or sexually explicit content. The Tournament Operator reserves the right to reject or request changes to any team name or logo.

### 3. Roster Rules

3.1 **Roster Size.** A team roster consists of five (5) core players and may include up to two (2) substitute players.

3.2 **Minimum Players.** A team must field a minimum of five (5) players in every match. If a team cannot field five players at match time, the match will be forfeited.

3.3 **Roster Lock.** Rosters are locked once the tournament goes live. After roster lock, only pre-registered substitutes may swap in for active players. New players cannot be added to the roster after this point.

3.4 **Substitutions.** A registered substitute may replace an active player between matches while the tournament is live. During an active match (server in progress), no roster swaps are permitted. In multi-map series (BO3/BO5), swaps are permitted during the inter-map break only. All substitutions are processed through the PlayCS platform.

3.5 **Three-Player Rule.** In accordance with Valve's Tournament Operating Requirements (Section 3.10.1), a roster must field at least three (3) of its registered core players in every match. Failure to do so will result in a forfeit.

3.6 **Conflict of Interest.** No player may be registered on more than one team in the same tournament. Players and team staff may not have any financial interest in the success of another team competing in the same tournament. Any known conflicts of interest must be disclosed to the Tournament Operator before the start of the tournament.

### 4. Code of Conduct & Integrity

4.1 **Sportsmanship.** All participants are expected to conduct themselves with respect towards other players, teams, tournament staff, spectators, and the broader community. This applies to behavior in-game, on the PlayCS platform, in voice communication channels, on social media, and at LAN venues.

4.2 **Prohibited Conduct.** The following conduct is strictly prohibited and may result in penalties up to and including permanent disqualification:

   a) **Cheating** — The use of any software, hardware modification, or exploit that provides an unfair competitive advantage. This includes but is not limited to aimbots, wallhacks, ESP, triggerbot, and any form of external assistance not provided by the game client.

   b) **Match Fixing** — Deliberately influencing the outcome of a match for reasons other than legitimate competitive success, including intentional losing.

   c) **Betting** — No participant, team staff, or team management may bet on any match in a tournament they are participating in, directly or indirectly.

   d) **Stream Sniping** — Watching or receiving information from any live broadcast of the match being played.

   e) **Information Abuse** — Receiving information about the game state from any external source not available to all players (e.g., spectators, stream delay exploits).

   f) **Harassment & Hate Speech** — Any form of harassment, discrimination, hate speech, threats, or abusive behavior based on race, ethnicity, gender, sexual orientation, religion, nationality, disability, or any other personal characteristic.

   g) **Impersonation** — Playing under a false identity or allowing another person to play on your account.

   h) **Collusion** — Any agreement between opposing teams or players to manipulate match outcomes.

4.3 **Reporting.** Participants who witness or are subject to any form of prohibited conduct should report it to the Tournament Operator via the PlayCS platform or by contacting tournament staff directly.

### 5. Penalties

5.1 **Types of Penalties.** The Tournament Operator may impose the following penalties for rule violations, individually or in combination:

   a) **Warning** — A formal notice that further violations will result in more severe penalties.

   b) **Match Forfeit** — The offending team is declared to have lost the match.

   c) **Prize Money Deduction** — A percentage of the team's prize money may be deducted.

   d) **Disqualification** — Removal from the tournament with forfeiture of all prizes.

   e) **Ban** — Temporary or permanent ban from future PlayCS tournaments.

5.2 **Severity.** Penalties are proportional to the severity of the violation. Repeat offenses will be punished more severely.

5.3 **Cheating Penalties.** Any participant found to be cheating will be immediately disqualified, forfeit all prizes, and receive a minimum two (2) year ban from all PlayCS competitions. The results of all matches involving the cheating participant will be voided.

5.4 **Match Fixing Penalties.** Match fixing will result in immediate disqualification, forfeiture of all prizes, and a minimum two (2) year ban from all PlayCS competitions.

### 6. Disputes & Protests

6.1 **Match Protests.** If a team wishes to protest the result of a match, they must submit the protest to the Tournament Operator within fifteen (15) minutes of the match's conclusion. Protests must include a clear description of the issue and any available evidence.

6.2 **Admin Decision.** The Tournament Operator's decision on all match-related matters is final and binding. Decisions made by tournament administrators during a match are not subject to appeal.

6.3 **Late Protests.** Protests submitted after the deadline will not be considered unless extraordinary circumstances prevented timely submission.

### 7. Prizes & Compensation

7.1 **Prize Pool.** The prize pool for each tournament is published on the tournament page on playcs.gg before registration opens. Prize distribution by placement is specified in the tournament details.

7.2 **Equal Treatment.** In accordance with Valve's Tournament Operating Requirements (Section 3.2.8), compensation for a given standing cannot exceed compensation for any higher standing, and compensation for equal standings must be equivalent.

7.3 **Payment.** Prize money will be distributed via GG Coins (platform currency) or USD (deposited to the player's PlayCS wallet), as specified in the tournament details. Prize distribution is processed automatically by the platform after the tournament concludes. For GG Coin prizes, premium/plus members receive their applicable multiplier bonus. The prize split mode (among players who played or all roster members) is configured per tournament.

7.4 **Minimum Completion.** Prizes will only be distributed if at least 50% of tournament matches have been completed. If this threshold is not met, the Tournament Operator will determine the appropriate course of action.

7.5 **Forfeiture.** Teams that are disqualified forfeit all accumulated prize money.

## Part 2: Competition Rules

### 8. Tournament Format & Scheduling

8.1 **Format.** Each tournament's format (Single Elimination, Double Elimination), match format (BO1, BO3, BO5), and structure (qualifiers, group stage, playoffs) are specified on the tournament page on playcs.gg.

8.2 **Event Structure.** PlayCS events may consist of multiple stages:
   - **Open Qualifiers** — Online, open registration
   - **Main Event** — Online or LAN
   - **Playoffs** — LAN (where specified)

8.3 **Schedule.** Match times are published on the tournament page. Players are responsible for monitoring the schedule and being available at the designated times.

8.4 **Punctuality.** All players must be connected to the match lobby and ready to play at the scheduled match time.

8.5 **Late Policy.** When a tournament match is created, both team captains must accept the match within the match ready window (default: 10 minutes). If a team does not accept within this window, the match is forfeited. Once both teams accept, team captains must confirm readiness to begin the veto within 60 seconds.

8.6 **Withdrawal.** Teams may withdraw from a tournament during the registration period. Once registration closes, withdrawal is no longer available through the platform. Entry fees (GG Coins and/or tournament passes) are refunded upon withdrawal during the registration period. Teams that fail to show up after registration closes will be treated as a forfeit.

### 9. Map Pool & Veto

9.1 **Map Pool.** The tournament map pool follows the current Valve Active Duty Map Group. If Valve updates the active duty map pool, the Tournament Operator may update the tournament map pool accordingly with advance notice to participants.

9.2 **Map Veto.** Map veto is conducted through the PlayCS platform. The veto procedure, timing, and sequence (ban/pick order) are automated and displayed in the match lobby.

9.3 **Veto Procedures.**

   **Best-of-One (BO1):**
   1. Team A bans one map
   2. Team B bans one map
   3. Team A bans one map
   4. Team B bans one map
   5. Team A bans one map
   6. Team B bans one map
   7. Remaining map is played; knife round determines starting sides

   **Best-of-Three (BO3):**
   1. Team A bans one map
   2. Team B bans one map
   3. Team A picks one map; Team B chooses starting side
   4. Team B picks one map; Team A chooses starting side
   5. Team A bans one map
   6. Team B bans one map
   7. Remaining map is the decider; knife round determines starting sides

   **Best-of-Five (BO5):**
   1. Team A bans one map
   2. Team B bans one map
   3. Team A picks one map; Team B chooses starting side
   4. Team B picks one map; Team A chooses starting side
   5. Team A picks one map; Team B chooses starting side
   6. Team B picks one map; Team A chooses starting side
   7. Remaining map is the decider; knife round determines starting sides

9.4 **Veto Timer.** Each team has 30 seconds per veto action (configurable per tournament). If a team fails to make a selection within the time limit, a random selection will be made automatically.

9.5 **Better Seed.** The higher-seeded team (as determined by the tournament seeding) decides whether they are Team A or Team B in the veto process.

### 10. Match Rules

10.1 **Match Settings.** All matches are played with standard competitive Counter-Strike 2 settings as configured on PlayCS servers:
   - Rounds: Best of 24 (MR12)
   - Round time: 1 minute 55 seconds
   - Freeze time: 18 seconds
   - Halftime duration: 15 seconds
   - Overtime: Best of 6 (MR3), $10,000 start money (repeated until winner determined)

10.2 **Server.** All online matches are played on servers provided through the PlayCS platform. Server region is determined by the tournament configuration (typically EU/Israel).

10.3 **Knife Round.** Where applicable (BO1 decider, BO3/BO5 final map), a knife round determines starting sides. The winning team of the knife round has 90 seconds to choose their preferred starting side (stay or switch). If no choice is made, the side is assigned randomly.

10.4 **Overtime.** If the match is tied after 24 regulation rounds, overtime is played. Teams remain on their current side at the start of overtime. Overtimes continue until a winner is determined.

10.5 **Match Breaks.** In multi-map series, a break between maps may be configured per tournament. The break duration is displayed in the match lobby. If no break is configured, the next map begins immediately after the veto/side selection for that map.

### 11. Anti-Cheat

11.1 **Akros Anti-Cheat.** When a tournament requires anti-cheat (as indicated on the tournament page), the Akros anti-cheat client is **mandatory** for all participants. Players must have Akros installed, running, and verified before joining a match lobby. The platform will enforce this requirement — players without an active Akros client will not be able to ready up.

11.2 **Failure to Run Anti-Cheat.** A player who cannot run Akros or refuses to do so is not eligible to participate. If a player's Akros client is not active during a match, the match may be paused, and the player will be given a reasonable time (up to 5 minutes) to resolve the issue. If the issue cannot be resolved, the team must substitute the player or forfeit.

11.3 **Detection & Consequences.** If Akros detects cheating software during a match, the player will be immediately removed and the team will forfeit the match. The player will be subject to penalties as outlined in Section 5.3.

11.4 **Additional Checks.** The Tournament Operator reserves the right to conduct additional anti-cheat reviews, including but not limited to demo review, server-side analysis, and manual inspection. Players may be required to submit POV demos upon request.

11.5 **LAN Anti-Cheat.** At LAN events, additional anti-cheat measures may apply, including hardware inspections and the use of tournament-provided equipment. Details will be communicated in the LAN-specific briefing.

### 12. Voice Communication

12.1 **Mandatory Voice Server.** During all matches, every team member actively playing must be connected to the designated PlayCS voice communication server (Discord or TeamSpeak, as specified for the tournament). Teams will be assigned a private channel.

12.2 **Communication Restrictions.** During a match, only the five (5) active players and one (1) registered coach (if applicable) may communicate in the team's voice channel. All other persons must remain muted if present.

12.3 **Recording.** The Tournament Operator reserves the right to record all voice communications during matches for integrity and broadcast purposes. By participating, players consent to such recordings.

12.4 **Technical Issues.** If a player experiences voice communication issues, they must notify a tournament administrator immediately. The team may request a technical pause to resolve the issue.

### 13. Bugs & Exploits

13.1 **General Rule.** The intentional use of any bugs, glitches, or errors in the game that provide an unfair competitive advantage is forbidden. The Tournament Operator determines whether the use of a bug affected the match and what penalties, if any, apply.

13.2 **Prohibited Exploits:**
   - Moving through clipped areas not intended by map design (walls, ceilings, floors)
   - Planting the bomb where it cannot be defused or where planting/beeping sounds are removed
   - Pixel walking (standing on invisible ledges with no visible edge)
   - Pushing a player's point of view through a wall or surface to gain visual information

13.3 **Permitted Mechanics:**
   - Defusing through walls or objects
   - Jump-throw binds
   - Silent drops
   - Surfing on surfaces
   - Boosting (except to positions where players can see through unintended areas)
   - `r_cleardecals` bind

13.4 **Scripts.** All scripts are prohibited except for buy binds, toggle binds, jump binds, and jump-throw binds. If a player is unsure whether a script or bind is permitted, they must contact the Tournament Operator before use.

13.5 **SOCD.** The use of Simultaneous Opposing Cardinal Directions (SOCD) features, including but not limited to Snap Tap, Snappy Tappy, or equivalent mechanisms, follows the current Valve policy. If Valve prohibits such features, they are prohibited in PlayCS tournaments.

### 14. Pauses & Timeouts

14.1 **Tactical Timeouts.** Each team is allowed three (3) tactical timeouts of 30 seconds each during regulation. Tactical timeouts are called via the in-game vote system. If the match reaches overtime, remaining timeouts are removed and each team receives one (1) timeout per overtime period.

14.2 **Technical Pauses.** If a player experiences a technical issue that prevents them from playing (e.g., crash, hardware failure, connection loss), the server will automatically trigger a technical pause. The automatic technical pause lasts up to **2 minutes**. If more time is needed, players may use the `.tech` command for a manual technical pause (up to **5 minutes** each, maximum 2 per team per map). The reason for any technical pause must be communicated to the tournament administrator immediately.

14.3 **Communication During Technical Pauses.** All game-related communication (tactical discussion) is **forbidden** during technical pauses. Only communication related to resolving the technical issue is permitted.

14.4 **Communication During Tactical Timeouts.** Full team communication is permitted during tactical timeouts, including with the registered coach.

14.5 **Abuse of Pauses.** Misuse of the technical pause function for non-technical reasons will result in the pause being ended and the offending team receiving a warning. Repeated abuse will result in further penalties.

### 15. Reconnecting & Match Interruptions

15.1 **Disconnection.** If a player disconnects during a live round, the round continues. The game should be paused at the next freeze time until the player reconnects or is substituted.

15.2 **Reconnect Time.** A disconnected player should reconnect as quickly as possible. In tournament matches, the server does not enforce an automatic disconnect timeout — the technical pause system (Section 14.2) governs the available time. If a player cannot reconnect within the technical pause budget, the team must substitute or continue with fewer players. If the team has fewer than 4 players, the match is forfeited.

15.3 **Server Crash.** If the server crashes, the match will be restored using the round backup system to the beginning of the round in which the crash occurred. If restoration is not possible, the Tournament Operator will determine the appropriate course of action.

15.4 **Match Restoration.** If a round must be restored:
   - If no significant gameplay has occurred, the round is replayed.
   - If damage has occurred and the round's outcome was determinable, it stands.
   - If the outcome was not determinable, the round is replayed from backup.

## Part 3: Online Match Rules

### 16. Online Environment

16.1 **Server Location.** Online matches are played on servers located in Europe (typically Frankfurt or Amsterdam), providing optimal connectivity for Israeli-based teams.

16.2 **Player Environment.** During an online match, only the five (5) active players (and one registered coach, if applicable) may be in the player's physical or virtual environment. No external assistance, coaching, or communication from non-team members is permitted.

16.3 **Stream Delay.** If the match is being streamed, a minimum delay will be applied. Regardless of any delay, participants are strictly forbidden from viewing any live or delayed broadcast of their own match.

16.4 **Camera Requirements.** When specified by the Tournament Operator, players may be required to provide a webcam feed during matches. Requirements will be communicated before the tournament.

## Part 4: LAN / Offline Match Rules

### 17. General LAN Rules

17.1 **Applicability.** This section applies to all matches designated as LAN/offline in the tournament schedule. All rules from Parts 1 and 2 continue to apply unless specifically overridden by this section.

17.2 **Venue Rules.** Participants must comply with all rules of the LAN venue (e.g., LAN shop, event hall) in addition to this rulebook. The Tournament Operator will communicate any venue-specific rules before the event.

17.3 **Attendance.** Players must arrive at the venue at the time specified by the Tournament Operator. Late arrival penalties follow Section 8.5.

### 18. Equipment

18.1 **BYOC (Bring Your Own Computer) Events.** When the event is designated as BYOC, players must bring:
   - Their own PC (meeting minimum system requirements)
   - Monitor
   - All peripherals (keyboard, mouse, mousepad, headset/in-ears)
   - Required cables and power adapters

18.2 **LAN Shop / Venue PC Events.** When the event uses venue-provided computers:
   - The Tournament Operator or venue provides PCs and monitors
   - Players must bring their own peripherals (keyboard, mouse, mousepad, headset/in-ears)
   - Players may not install unauthorized software on venue PCs
   - Players may not modify BIOS settings on venue PCs

18.3 **Peripheral Inspection.** The Tournament Operator reserves the right to inspect any player equipment for unauthorized modifications. Players may be asked to temporarily surrender equipment for inspection.

18.4 **Equipment Failure.** Players are responsible for the functioning of their own equipment. If a player's equipment fails, a technical pause may be called (see Section 14.2). The Tournament Operator is not obligated to provide replacement equipment.

### 19. Playing Area

19.1 **Access.** During a match, only the five (5) active players and one (1) registered coach (if applicable) are permitted in the designated playing area. All other team staff, substitutes, and spectators must remain outside the playing area.

19.2 **Electronic Devices.** Mobile phones, tablets, smartwatches, and any electronic devices not required for gameplay must be turned off or surrendered to tournament staff before the match begins. Players requiring a phone for Steam Guard authentication may briefly use it under supervision of a tournament official.

19.3 **Coach Communication.** At LAN events, coaches may only communicate with players during tactical timeouts and halftime/side switches. Coaches must not communicate (verbally, by gesture, or any other means) with players during live rounds.

19.4 **Documents.** Players may bring physical documents (notepads, printouts) for tactical purposes into the playing area.

19.5 **Food & Drink.** Food must be kept under the table and out of sight. Drinks must be in closed containers. The Tournament Operator may remove any food or drink from the playing area if deemed necessary.

### 20. Setup & Warm-Up

20.1 **Setup Period.** A setup period of at least thirty (30) minutes will be provided before the first match of each day. This covers peripheral setup, settings configuration, and warm-up.

20.2 **For BYOC Events:** Players should arrive with their systems pre-configured. Additional time for troubleshooting hardware issues is at the discretion of the Tournament Operator.

20.3 **For Venue PC Events:** Players will have access to configure their game settings (video, audio, keybinds, crosshair) during the setup period. Players are encouraged to have their CS2 configuration files on a USB drive for quick setup.

20.4 **Equal Access.** Both teams in a match will be given equal setup time. One team will not be permitted to begin setup before the other unless the delay is caused by the late team's own actions.

## Part 5: Additional Provisions

### 21. Data Verifiability

21.1 In accordance with Valve's Tournament Operating Requirements (Section 4.4), all tournament information — including this rulebook, tournament announcements, and registration data — is published on platforms that preserve version history or are independently archived.

21.2 Registration timestamps and order are publicly visible on the PlayCS tournament page.

21.3 All match results, brackets, and match data are recorded and publicly accessible through the PlayCS platform.

### 22. Non-Exclusivity

22.1 In accordance with Valve's Tournament Operating Requirements (Section 5.1), PlayCS does not engage in direct or indirect agreements with teams, rosters, or players that would artificially restrict their ability to participate in other tournaments. Players and teams are free to compete in any other tournament or league.

22.2 PlayCS may only restrict a participant's involvement in other tournaments while they are on location at a PlayCS LAN event and within the published dates of that event.

### 23. Disqualification Transparency

23.1 In accordance with Valve's Tournament Operating Requirements (Section 5.4), the criteria for disqualification are published in this rulebook (Sections 4 and 5). Disqualification rules are applied equally to all participants based on objective criteria, regardless of team identity.

23.2 If a team or player is disqualified from a tournament, PlayCS will publish the details of the disqualification decision at the time it occurs, including the rule(s) violated and the penalty imposed.

### 24. Amendments

24.1 The Tournament Operator reserves the right to amend this rulebook at any time. Changes will be communicated to participants via the PlayCS platform.

24.2 The version of this rulebook in effect at the time of a tournament's Additional Information announcement applies to that tournament. All versions of this rulebook are maintained with full version history on GitHub at https://github.com/playcs-gg/rulebook.
